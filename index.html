US Clinical & Regulatory Landscape

ADHD diagnosis in the US follows DSM-5 criteria (APA): persistent inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity across 2+ settings, with onset before age 12. Standard clinical tools include ASRS (Adult Self-Report Scale), DIVA 5.0, Conners scales, and CAARS. A comprehensive evaluation typically involves structured clinical interview, validated rating scales, developmental history, and rule-out of differential diagnoses.

Who Can Diagnose & Prescribe

  • Diagnose: Psychiatrists (MD/DO), psychologists (PhD/PsyD), NPs, PAs, and PCPs
  • Prescribe stimulants: MD, DO, NP, PA â€” all require individual DEA registration
  • ADHD medications are Schedule II controlled substances (Adderall, Ritalin, Vyvanse, Concerta)
  • No auto-refills: new prescription required every 30-90 days
  • Prescribers must check state PDMP (Prescription Drug Monitoring Program) before each script

Telehealth Prescribing Rules

  • Ryan Haight Act: Normally requires in-person visit before prescribing controlled substances via telehealth
  • COVID waivers allowed telehealth-only prescribing; DEA has extended flexibilities through 2025-2026
  • DEA proposed rule: established patient relationship via telehealth may be sufficient for ongoing prescriptions
  • Mentalwell advantage: comprehensive assessment exceeds minimum diagnostic requirements, positioning well for any final DEA rules
